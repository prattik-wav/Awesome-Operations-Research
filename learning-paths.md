## Undergraduate

- Books: Hillier and Lieberman, Taha, Winston, and Bradley/Hax/Magnanti.
- Courses: MIT 15.053, NPTEL Operations Research, and introductory algorithms.
- Software: Excel Solver, OpenSolver, PuLP, OR-Tools, and Pyomo.
- Projects: product mix LP, transportation model, assignment model, shortest path model, basic queue simulation, and inventory newsvendor analysis.

## Master's

- Books: Bertsimas and Tsitsiklis, Nemhauser and Wolsey, Law simulation text, and Pinedo scheduling.
- Courses: convex optimization, integer programming, stochastic models, simulation, and an application seminar.
- Software: Pyomo, JuMP, Gurobi or CPLEX, HiGHS, OR-Tools, SimPy, and MiniZinc.
- Projects: facility location, vehicle routing, production scheduling, simulation output analysis, and stochastic inventory model.

## PhD

- Books: Schrijver, Rockafellar, Nocedal and Wright, Shapiro/Dentcheva/Ruszczynski, Bertsekas, and Puterman.
- Courses: polyhedral theory, convex analysis, stochastic programming, dynamic programming, complexity, decomposition, and research seminars.
- Software: solver APIs, Julia/JuMP, Pyomo, SCIP, custom algorithms, benchmarking scripts, and reproducibility tooling.
- Projects: reproduce a paper, implement decomposition, test cuts or heuristics, build benchmark infrastructure, and write a publishable computational study.

## Industry Practitioner

- Books: Model Building in Mathematical Programming, Data, Models, and Decisions, and domain-specific OR books.
- Courses: MIT 15.053, Analytics Edge, Gurobi modeling examples, and Pyomo/JuMP tutorials.
- Software: Excel/OpenSolver for communication, Python or Julia for production models, commercial solvers for reliability, and dashboards for deployment.
- Projects: decision-support prototype, sensitivity analysis, scenario planning, solver-log interpretation, and stakeholder-facing model documentation.

## Data Scientist

- Books: Convex Optimization, Algorithms for Optimization, and decision-focused learning papers.
- Courses: convex optimization, machine learning, reinforcement learning, and analytics courses.
- Software: CVXPY, PyTorch/JAX, scikit-learn, OR-Tools, Pyomo, and MLflow-style experiment tracking.
- Projects: predict-then-optimize workflow, decision-focused loss, portfolio optimization, contextual bandit, and inventory policy simulation.

## Supply Chain Analyst

- Books: Chopra, Simchi-Levi, Zipkin, and Talluri/van Ryzin for revenue management.
- Courses: MIT CTL materials, supply chain analytics, inventory theory, and network optimization.
- Software: Pyomo, JuMP, Gurobi, CPLEX, OR-Tools, AnyLogic, SimPy, and GIS tools.
- Projects: network design, safety stock optimization, routing, warehouse simulation, S&OP planning, and disruption response.

## Optimization Engineer

- Books: Numerical Optimization, Integer Programming, Convex Optimization, and solver documentation.
- Courses: convex optimization, integer programming, algorithms, numerical linear algebra, and software engineering.
- Software: C++ or Rust for performance-critical services, Python/Julia for modeling, solver APIs, containerization, and benchmark harnesses.
- Projects: model generation service, solver parameter tuning, decomposition framework, warm-start pipeline, and production monitoring for optimization jobs.
