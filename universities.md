## How to Evaluate an OR Program

- Faculty fit - Look for faculty publishing in the OR subfields you want to study.
- Methods depth - Strong programs usually cover optimization, stochastic systems, simulation, algorithms, and computation.
- Application breadth - Look for labs in supply chain, healthcare, energy, transportation, finance, or public policy.
- Software culture - Check whether courses use Pyomo, JuMP, OR-Tools, AMPL, GAMS, CPLEX, Gurobi, or simulation tools.
- Research seminars - Active seminar series are a good signal of research community strength.
- Industry connections - Capstone projects, internships, research centers, and alumni networks matter for practitioners.
