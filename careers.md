## Operations Research Analyst

- What the role does: builds mathematical models, evaluates operational decisions, runs scenarios, and communicates recommendations.
- Core methods: LP, MIP, simulation, forecasting, queueing, decision analysis, and sensitivity analysis.
- Programming/software skills: Python, SQL, Excel, Pyomo or PuLP, OR-Tools, Gurobi or CPLEX, and visualization tools.
- Recommended learning path: [Undergraduate](learning-paths.md#undergraduate), [Master's](learning-paths.md#masters), and [Operations Research Curriculum](or-curriculum.md).
- Recommended resources: [Books](books.md), [Courses](courses.md), [Software](softwares.md), and [Methodologies](methodologies.md).
- Typical employers: logistics firms, airlines, consultancies, government agencies, healthcare systems, retailers, and technology companies.
- Portfolio ideas: transportation model, facility location model, queueing simulation, and decision-support dashboard.

## Operations Analyst

- What the role does: analyzes operational performance, identifies process bottlenecks, builds dashboards, and supports tactical decisions.
- Core methods: descriptive analytics, forecasting, simulation, basic optimization, process analysis, and statistics.
- Programming/software skills: SQL, Excel, Python or R, BI tools, spreadsheet optimization, and basic simulation.
- Recommended learning path: [Industry Practitioner](learning-paths.md#industry-practitioner).
- Typical employers: operations teams, manufacturers, retailers, healthcare systems, logistics companies, and SaaS operations groups.
- Portfolio ideas: KPI dashboard, capacity analysis, service-level simulation, and staffing model.

## Optimization Engineer

- What the role does: designs, implements, tests, deploys, and monitors optimization systems in production.
- Core methods: LP, MIP, CP, decomposition, heuristics, solver tuning, numerical diagnostics, and algorithm engineering.
- Programming/software skills: Python, Julia, C++ or Java, Pyomo/JuMP, solver APIs, Docker, CI, cloud deployment, and observability.
- Recommended learning path: [Optimization Engineer](learning-paths.md#optimization-engineer), [Solver Comparison](solver-comparison.md), and [Modeling Language Comparison](modeling-languages.md).
- Typical employers: technology companies, logistics platforms, airlines, energy firms, supply chain software companies, and OR consultancies.
- Portfolio ideas: production-style routing service, solver benchmark harness, model validation suite, and decomposition prototype.

## Supply Chain Scientist

- What the role does: improves supply chain planning, inventory, network design, fulfillment, procurement, and resilience.
- Core methods: MIP, inventory theory, stochastic programming, facility location, routing, forecasting, and simulation.
- Programming/software skills: Python, SQL, Pyomo/JuMP, Gurobi/CPLEX, BI tools, and supply chain planning systems.
- Recommended learning path: [Supply Chain Analyst](learning-paths.md#supply-chain-analyst) and [Supply Chain and Logistics](industry-guides.md#supply-chain-and-logistics).
- Typical employers: retailers, manufacturers, e-commerce companies, logistics providers, consultancies, and software vendors.
- Portfolio ideas: supply chain network design, inventory simulator, warehouse slotting optimizer, and last-mile routing model.

## Revenue Management Analyst

- What the role does: designs pricing, booking, assortment, and capacity-allocation decisions under uncertain demand.
- Core methods: dynamic programming, forecasting, stochastic optimization, choice modeling, simulation, and experimentation.
- Programming/software skills: SQL, Python/R, optimization modeling, statistical modeling, experimentation, and visualization.
- Recommended resources: [Finance and Revenue Management](industry-guides.md#finance-and-revenue-management), [AI and OR](ai-and-or.md), and [Revenue Management books](books.md#applied-or-and-analytics).
- Typical employers: airlines, hotels, retail, e-commerce, marketplaces, entertainment, and transportation companies.
- Portfolio ideas: dynamic pricing simulator, booking-limit model, assortment optimizer, and demand unconstraining analysis.

## Simulation Consultant

- What the role does: builds simulation models to evaluate systems, capacity, layouts, policies, and operational risk.
- Core methods: discrete-event simulation, Monte Carlo simulation, input modeling, output analysis, validation, and ranking/selection.
- Programming/software skills: SimPy, Arena, Simio, AnyLogic, Python/R, statistics, experiment design, and visualization.
- Recommended resources: [Simulation](methodologies.md#simulation), [Simulation Software](softwares.md#simulation-software), and [Simulation Tutorials](tutorials.md#simulation-tutorials).
- Typical employers: consulting firms, hospitals, manufacturers, logistics companies, defense contractors, and infrastructure planners.
- Portfolio ideas: hospital bed simulation, warehouse process simulation, call-center model, and manufacturing line simulation.

## Decision Scientist

- What the role does: connects analytics, causal reasoning, optimization, experimentation, and stakeholder decisions.
- Core methods: decision analysis, optimization, causal inference, simulation, Bayesian analysis, and prescriptive analytics.
- Programming/software skills: Python/R, SQL, experiment analysis, optimization libraries, dashboards, and communication.
- Recommended resources: [Decision Analysis](methodologies.md#decision-analysis), [AI and OR](ai-and-or.md#decision-intelligence), and [Best Of](best-of.md).
- Typical employers: tech companies, marketplaces, consulting firms, healthcare, finance, and public policy groups.
- Portfolio ideas: value-of-information analysis, decision-focused forecasting model, and policy simulator.

## Quantitative Analyst

- What the role does: develops quantitative models for finance, risk, pricing, portfolios, and trading decisions.
- Core methods: convex optimization, stochastic processes, robust optimization, simulation, statistics, and time series.
- Programming/software skills: Python, R, SQL, CVXPY, MOSEK, Gurobi, pandas, numerical computing, and visualization.
- Recommended resources: [Finance and Revenue Management](industry-guides.md#finance-and-revenue-management), [Convex Optimization](methodologies.md#convex-optimization), and [Stochastic Programming](methodologies.md#stochastic-programming).
- Typical employers: banks, asset managers, hedge funds, fintech companies, insurers, and risk teams.
- Portfolio ideas: portfolio optimizer, risk simulation, robust asset allocation, and transaction-cost-aware rebalancing model.

## Industrial Engineer

- What the role does: improves production, service systems, human work, quality, layout, and operational processes.
- Core methods: simulation, scheduling, facility layout, quality engineering, queueing, ergonomics, and optimization.
- Programming/software skills: Excel, Python/R, simulation software, statistical analysis, MIP tools, and process mapping.
- Recommended resources: [Manufacturing and Production](industry-guides.md#manufacturing-and-production), [Teaching](teaching.md), and [Software](softwares.md).
- Typical employers: manufacturers, hospitals, logistics firms, warehouses, consultancies, and government agencies.
- Portfolio ideas: production scheduler, line-balancing model, layout analysis, and queueing-based staffing model.

## Research Scientist

- What the role does: develops new methods, publishes research, prototypes algorithms, and transfers ideas to practice.
- Core methods: depends on subfield; often optimization, stochastic systems, simulation, ML, algorithms, and computational experiments.
- Programming/software skills: Python/Julia/C++, solver APIs, reproducible research, Git, containers, experiment tracking, and technical writing.
- Recommended resources: [Research Guide](research-guide.md), [Papers](papers.md), [Journals](journals.md), [Researchers](researchers.md), and [Universities](universities.md).
- Typical employers: universities, research labs, tech companies, solver companies, national labs, and advanced analytics groups.
- Portfolio ideas: reproducible paper replication, benchmark study, open-source solver contribution, and research preprint.

## Data Scientist with OR Focus

- What the role does: combines prediction with optimization and simulation to improve operational decisions.
- Core methods: forecasting, ML, optimization, simulation, causal inference, decision-focused learning, and experimentation.
- Programming/software skills: Python, SQL, scikit-learn, PyTorch/JAX, CVXPY, OR-Tools, Pyomo/JuMP, and visualization.
- Recommended resources: [Data Scientist](learning-paths.md#data-scientist), [AI and OR](ai-and-or.md), and [Libraries](libraries.md).
- Typical employers: technology, retail, logistics, marketplace, healthcare, and finance companies.
- Portfolio ideas: predict-then-optimize pipeline, contextual inventory decisions, and routing model with demand forecasts.

## Logistics Analyst

- What the role does: improves transportation, routing, warehousing, fleet use, and delivery performance.
- Core methods: routing, network optimization, facility location, MIP, heuristics, simulation, and forecasting.
- Programming/software skills: SQL, Python, GIS, OR-Tools, Pyomo, BI tools, and spreadsheet modeling.
- Recommended resources: [Transportation and Routing](industry-guides.md#transportation-and-routing) and [Vehicle Routing and Logistics](methodologies.md#vehicle-routing-and-logistics).
- Typical employers: 3PLs, retailers, carriers, e-commerce companies, manufacturers, and public agencies.
- Portfolio ideas: vehicle routing optimizer, delivery zone design, warehouse throughput model, and fleet sizing model.

## Healthcare Operations Analyst

- What the role does: improves patient access, staffing, capacity, flow, scheduling, and resource use.
- Core methods: queueing, simulation, scheduling, MIP, stochastic modeling, and decision analysis.
- Programming/software skills: SQL, Python/R, SimPy or commercial simulation, Pyomo/JuMP, dashboards, and healthcare data literacy.
- Recommended resources: [Healthcare Operations Research](industry-guides.md#healthcare-operations-research) and [Healthcare Operations](applications.md#healthcare-operations).
- Typical employers: hospitals, health systems, insurers, public health agencies, consulting firms, and health tech companies.
- Portfolio ideas: emergency department simulation, appointment scheduling model, bed capacity dashboard, and staffing optimizer.

## Energy Systems Optimization Analyst

- What the role does: models energy planning, dispatch, power flow, storage, renewables, and market operations.
- Core methods: MIP, NLP, stochastic programming, robust optimization, decomposition, and simulation.
- Programming/software skills: Python, Julia, Pyomo, JuMP, PowerModels.jl, MATPOWER, Gurobi, Ipopt, and data engineering.
- Recommended resources: [Energy Systems](industry-guides.md#energy-systems), [Datasets](datasets.md#energy-systems-optimization-datasets), and [Solver Comparison](solver-comparison.md).
- Typical employers: utilities, grid operators, renewable developers, energy analytics companies, consultancies, and labs.
- Portfolio ideas: economic dispatch model, unit commitment prototype, storage arbitrage model, and optimal power flow benchmark.

## Portfolio Project Ideas

- Vehicle routing optimizer - Build a small CVRP or VRPTW model with OR-Tools or a MIP solver.
- Hospital bed capacity simulation - Use SimPy or Arena-style logic to evaluate staffing and bed policies.
- Inventory policy simulator - Compare EOQ, newsvendor, and base-stock policies under stochastic demand.
- Airline crew scheduling prototype - Build a simplified set covering or set partitioning model.
- Warehouse slotting optimizer - Assign SKUs to locations based on demand, travel time, and capacity.
- Energy dispatch model - Model economic dispatch or unit commitment with a small power system dataset.
- Supply chain network design model - Choose facilities and flows under demand and cost scenarios.
- Workforce scheduling model - Create shifts and assignments under coverage and labor rules.
- Portfolio optimization model - Compare mean-variance and robust portfolio formulations.
- Decision-focused ML project - Train a prediction model and evaluate downstream optimization quality.
